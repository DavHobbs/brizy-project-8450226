Don't Ignore RTR Notices

At Equity Legal LLP, we are San Diego based real estate attorneys, licensed and insured real estate brokers, and property developers with over 22 years of expert experience.

Increased Second Mortgage Balance:

Failure to address RTR notices can lead to the accumulation of additional fees and penalties, resulting in a higher balance on your second mortgage.

Home Sale Blockage:

RTR can impede the sale of your home, making it difficult to transfer ownership or find buyers.

Refinancing Restrictions:

By neglecting RTR notices, you may encounter obstacles when attempting to refinance your loan, potentially missing out on better terms or lower interest rates.

Stripped Equity:

In the worst-case scenario, inaction on RTR notices can lead to foreclosure, resulting in the loss of all the equity you've built in your home.
